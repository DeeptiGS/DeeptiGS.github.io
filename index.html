Cyclistic Analysis

Welcome to the Cyclistic bike-share analysis case study!

This is a Capstone Project as part of Google Data Analytics Certificate. The case study focuses on a fictional bike-share company ‘Cyclistic‘, based in Chicago. The company features more than 5,800 bicycles and 600 docking stations across Chicago.

Cyclistic’s finance analysts have concluded that annual members are much more profitable than casual riders. Although the pricing flexibility helps Cyclistic attract more customers, Moreno(The director of marketing and Manager) believes that maximizing the number of annual members will be key to future growth. Rather than creating a marketing campaign that targets all-new customers, Moreno believes there is a very good chance to convert casual riders into members. She notes that casual riders are already aware of the Cyclistic program and have chosen Cyclistic for their mobility needs. The goal of the case study is to design marketing strategies aimed at converting casual riders into annual members. In order to do that, I have done a detailed analysis of the data to better understand how annual members and casual riders differ.

In this project Cyclistic’s historical trip data of the past 12 months(August 2020- July 2021) was used to analyze and identify the trends. The data has been publicly made available by Motivate International Inc. under this license.

Since the dataset was large, I have used Python to prepare, process, analyze and visualize the data.
